    b) It is a probabilistic classifier that's built on Bayes' Theorem. The model makes one key assumption: that all your features are independent from each other, which is why it's called 'naive.' This assumption lets model train really fast and handle lots of features well, especially for things like text classification, even when you don't have tons of data. The problem is that features are rarely independent in real life. When your variables are actually related to each other, Naive Bayes starts to lose accuracy and the probability estimates it gives you aren't very reliable. Naive Bayes is quicker and easier to work with, but it doesn't handle complex relationships between variables as well. It's easier to understand than KNN, but it can't capture feature interactions like decision trees can.
